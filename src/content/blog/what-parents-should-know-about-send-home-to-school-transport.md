When your child has Special Educational Needs and Disabilities, the daily school journey can feel like one more thing to worry about. Understanding how home-to-school transport works, what your rights are and what to look for in a provider can help you feel more confident about the service your family relies on.

This guide covers the practical information parents need, drawn from over 18 years of experience delivering SEND transport across Birmingham, Manchester and surrounding areas.

## How SEND Transport Is Arranged

In most cases, home-to-school transport for SEND students is arranged and funded by the local authority. If your child has an Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP) and attends a school that is not within walking distance, the council is typically responsible for ensuring suitable transport is provided.

The local authority may operate its own vehicles or, more commonly, contract the service to specialist transport providers. These providers are selected based on their ability to meet strict safety, compliance and quality standards.

**Parents do not usually need to arrange transport directly.** The council's transport team will assess your child's needs, determine the appropriate type of vehicle and assign a route. However, understanding the process means you can ask the right questions and advocate effectively for your child.

## What to Expect From the Service

A well-run SEND transport service should provide consistency and reliability. Students often travel with the same driver and passenger assistant each day, which helps build familiarity and trust. This is particularly important for students with autism or anxiety, where changes in routine can cause significant distress.

**Vehicles** should be clean, well-maintained and appropriate for your child's needs. If your child uses a wheelchair, the vehicle must be fitted with approved restraint systems and a suitable ramp or lift. If your child has sensory needs, the environment inside the vehicle should be calm and managed.

**Passenger assistants** (sometimes called escorts) play a crucial role. They are responsible for the welfare of students during the journey, managing behaviour, administering any agreed support and communicating with both parents and schools. A good passenger assistant builds a relationship with each student and understands their individual needs.

**Timings** should be communicated clearly. You should know the expected pick-up and drop-off windows, and the provider should have a process for notifying you of any delays or changes.

## Safety Standards to Look For

Safety is understandably the top priority for parents. Here are the key areas to be aware of when evaluating your child's transport provision.

**DBS checks** are mandatory for all drivers and passenger assistants working with students. Every member of staff who has contact with your child should hold an enhanced DBS certificate. Do not hesitate to ask your local authority or the transport provider to confirm this.

**Vehicle maintenance** should follow a rigorous schedule. Vehicles used for SEND transport undergo regular inspections beyond the standard MOT, including daily walkaround checks by drivers. Providers who operate in-house maintenance workshops can often respond to issues more quickly than those who rely on external garages.

**GPS tracking and CCTV** are increasingly standard in SEND transport. GPS allows operators to monitor routes in real time and respond to delays, while CCTV provides a record of what happens during each journey. Both of these systems offer reassurance to parents and accountability for providers.

**Insurance and licensing** must be current and appropriate. Vehicles should hold the correct operator licences, and the provider should carry public liability insurance that covers passenger transport.

## Your Rights as a Parent

Local authorities have a legal duty to provide suitable transport for eligible SEND students. If you believe the transport provided is not meeting your child's needs, you have the right to raise concerns.

Start by contacting the council's transport team directly. Document specific issues, including dates, times and descriptions of what happened. If the issue is not resolved, you can escalate through the council's formal complaints process.

You also have the right to request information about the provider, including their compliance record and the qualifications of staff working with your child. Transparency should be expected, not requested.

## How to Support a Smooth Journey

While the transport provider is responsible for delivering a safe and reliable service, there are practical steps parents can take to help things run smoothly.

**Share relevant information** with the provider about your child's needs, preferences and any triggers that might affect their journey. The more the driver and passenger assistant understand about your child, the better they can support them.

**Establish a routine** at home around pick-up and drop-off times. Predictability helps students feel settled, and being ready on time reduces pressure on the overall route schedule.

**Communicate proactively** with the transport team. If your child is unwell, on holiday or has a change of circumstances, let the provider know as early as possible. This helps them plan routes efficiently and ensures no vehicle makes an unnecessary stop.

**Talk to your child** about their journey. Ask how they feel about the people in the vehicle and whether anything is bothering them. Students may not always volunteer information, so gentle, regular check-ins can surface issues early.

## Choosing Quality Over Convenience

Not all transport providers deliver the same standard of service. When councils select providers, they should be weighing experience, fleet quality, staff training and compliance history alongside cost. As a parent, you can advocate for this by asking your local authority about the criteria they use when awarding transport contracts.

Providers with established track records, in-house training programmes and dedicated fleet maintenance capabilities are more likely to deliver the consistent, safe service that SEND students need and deserve.
