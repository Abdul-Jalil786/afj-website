Behind every safe and successful journey for a vulnerable passenger, there is often a passenger assistant working quietly and professionally to ensure everything goes smoothly. These individuals are the unsung professionals of the transport sector, providing essential support to students with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities, patients travelling to medical appointments and other passengers who need additional assistance during their journey.

Despite the critical nature of their role, passenger assistants are frequently overlooked in discussions about transport quality. Understanding what they do and why their training matters is important for anyone involved in commissioning, delivering or using specialist transport services.

## What Passenger Assistants Do

A passenger assistant, sometimes referred to as an escort or attendant, travels in the vehicle alongside passengers to provide support and ensure their welfare throughout the journey. Their responsibilities are wide-ranging and vary depending on the type of service being delivered.

**In SEND transport,** passenger assistants are responsible for the safety and wellbeing of students from the moment they board the vehicle until they are handed over to a responsible adult at their destination. This includes helping students board and alight safely, securing wheelchair restraints, managing behaviour, providing reassurance to anxious students and communicating with parents and school staff at pick-up and drop-off points.

For students with complex needs, the passenger assistant may need to administer agreed interventions, use communication aids, manage sensory environments within the vehicle and respond to medical emergencies. The role demands a combination of practical skills, emotional intelligence and the ability to remain calm under pressure.

**In patient transport,** passenger assistants support patients who may be elderly, frail or managing acute health conditions. They assist with boarding and alighting, monitor patient comfort during the journey, communicate with clinical staff at healthcare facilities and ensure that any medical equipment travelling with the patient is properly secured.

**In private hire and group transport,** passenger assistants may support passengers with mobility challenges, provide information about the journey and ensure that all passengers are safely seated and secured before departure.

## The Human Element

What distinguishes an effective passenger assistant from someone who simply occupies a seat in the vehicle is the quality of the human connection they build with their passengers.

For SEND students, the passenger assistant is often one of the most consistent figures in their daily routine. The same assistant may accompany a student on every journey for an entire academic year or longer. Over time, this consistency allows the assistant to develop a deep understanding of the student's needs, preferences and triggers.

This relationship has tangible benefits. A student who trusts their passenger assistant is more likely to feel calm during the journey, more willing to communicate any discomfort or concerns and better prepared for the school day ahead. Conversely, a frequent change of passenger assistants can cause significant distress for students who rely on routine and familiarity.

The same principle applies in patient transport. A passenger assistant who takes the time to greet a patient warmly, explain what will happen during the journey and check on their comfort is providing a form of care that extends beyond simple logistics. For patients who are anxious about their medical appointment, this kind of attentive support can genuinely improve their experience.

## Why Training Matters

The breadth of the passenger assistant role means that proper training is not optional. It is a fundamental requirement for safe and effective service delivery.

**Safeguarding training** ensures that passenger assistants can recognise the signs of abuse or neglect and know how to report concerns through the appropriate channels. This is particularly critical in SEND transport, where students may be unable to communicate their experiences verbally.

**First aid certification** equips passenger assistants to respond to medical emergencies during a journey. This includes managing seizures, allergic reactions, choking and other incidents that may occur without warning.

**Manual handling training** is essential for assistants who support passengers with mobility challenges. Proper technique protects both the passenger and the assistant from injury during boarding, alighting and wheelchair transfers.

**Behaviour management training** provides strategies for supporting passengers who may exhibit challenging behaviour. This is particularly relevant in SEND transport, where students may experience meltdowns, anxiety episodes or sensory overload during their journey. Effective training focuses on de-escalation, understanding triggers and maintaining a calm environment.

**Disability awareness training** broadens the assistant's understanding of the conditions and needs they will encounter. This includes autism awareness, communication strategies for non-verbal passengers, epilepsy awareness and understanding the impact of sensory processing differences.

## Recruitment and Retention

Finding and retaining skilled passenger assistants is one of the transport sector's persistent challenges. The role demands a particular combination of patience, empathy, physical capability and resilience, and the pay often does not reflect the complexity of the work.

Providers who invest in their passenger assistants through competitive pay, genuine career development opportunities and a supportive working environment are more likely to retain experienced staff. This continuity directly benefits passengers, who receive a more consistent and higher-quality service.

High staff turnover, on the other hand, disrupts the relationships that vulnerable passengers depend on and creates ongoing recruitment and training costs that erode service quality over time.

## Recognising the Value

Passenger assistants deserve greater recognition for the vital role they play in specialist transport. They are not simply an additional cost line on a contract. They are the professionals who ensure that every passenger travels safely, comfortably and with dignity.

For councils commissioning transport services, the quality and training of a provider's passenger assistant workforce should be a key evaluation criterion. For families, knowing that a trained, DBS-checked and experienced assistant will be accompanying their child or relative on every journey provides essential peace of mind.

The vehicles and the routes are important, but it is the people inside them who truly define the quality of the service.
