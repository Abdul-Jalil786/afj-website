The summer holiday period is the most important maintenance window in the calendar for any transport operator delivering home-to-school services. While students are away from school, operators have a narrow but critical opportunity to bring their entire fleet up to peak condition before the demands of a new academic year begin.

For providers managing large fleets across multiple contracts, summer maintenance is not a quiet period. It is one of the busiest and most strategically important phases of the year.

## Why Summer Maintenance Matters

During term time, SEND transport vehicles are in constant daily use. A fleet of 200 vehicles completing morning and afternoon school runs accumulates significant wear across brakes, tyres, suspension, ramps and interior fittings. While routine maintenance continues throughout the year, the summer break provides the only extended window where vehicles can be taken off the road for more comprehensive work without disrupting services.

The consequences of underinvesting in summer maintenance become apparent quickly once September arrives. Breakdowns during school runs cause delays, require emergency vehicle swaps and create stress for students, families and staff. For students with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities, a disrupted journey can have a disproportionate impact on their day.

Proactive summer maintenance is therefore not just about mechanical reliability. It is about service quality, student welfare and contract compliance.

## The Summer Maintenance Programme

A well-planned summer maintenance programme covers several categories of work, each with its own priorities and timelines.

**Full vehicle inspections** go beyond the standard MOT. Every vehicle should receive a bumper-to-bumper inspection covering mechanical systems, electrical components, bodywork, interior condition and safety equipment. This inspection identifies issues that may not yet be causing problems but could develop into failures during term time.

**Brake and suspension work** is among the most common summer maintenance tasks. Vehicles used for school transport operate in stop-start urban environments, which places heavy demands on braking systems. Replacing worn brake pads, discs and suspension components during the summer prevents mid-term failures.

**Tyre replacement and alignment** ensures that every vehicle starts the new year with adequate tread depth and correct wheel alignment. This is particularly important for larger vehicles such as minibuses and wheelchair-accessible vehicles, where uneven tyre wear can affect handling and passenger comfort.

**Ramp and lift servicing** is essential for vehicles used in SEND transport. Wheelchair ramps and tail lifts are deployed multiple times per day during term time, and the mechanisms require regular lubrication, adjustment and component replacement. A ramp failure during a school run is a serious operational issue that can leave a wheelchair user unable to board.

**Interior deep cleaning and refurbishment** addresses the wear that accumulates over a full academic year. Seats are cleaned or reupholstered, flooring is checked for damage, seatbelts are tested and CCTV systems are verified. The interior condition of a vehicle affects both hygiene and the comfort of students who may spend significant time travelling each day.

## Compliance and Documentation

Summer maintenance is also a critical period for ensuring fleet-wide compliance with regulatory requirements. Transport operators must maintain detailed records of all maintenance work, and these records are subject to scrutiny during audits by the Traffic Commissioner, local authorities and the CQC (for patient transport vehicles).

**MOT scheduling** is reviewed during the summer to ensure that no vehicle's MOT expires during term time without a replacement booking in place. Operators managing large fleets need a rolling MOT calendar that accounts for every vehicle's test date and the lead time required for any remedial work.

**Licensing and insurance** documentation is checked and renewed where necessary. Every vehicle must hold the correct operator licence, and insurance policies must cover the specific types of transport being delivered.

**DBS and training records** for drivers and passenger assistants are also reviewed during the summer, although this falls outside the mechanical maintenance programme. Ensuring that all staff certifications are current before the new term begins avoids last-minute compliance gaps.

## The Advantage of In-House Workshops

Operators who rely entirely on external garages for maintenance face a significant challenge during the summer. Commercial garages are busy with their own seasonal workloads, and turnaround times can be unpredictable. A delay in getting a vehicle back from an external repairer can leave an operator short of vehicles when September arrives.

In-house maintenance workshops offer a decisive advantage. By managing repairs and servicing internally, an operator can prioritise work based on its own schedules and contract requirements. Urgent jobs can be fast-tracked, and quality control is maintained throughout the process.

At AFJ Limited, our in-house workshop handles the full range of fleet maintenance, from routine servicing to major mechanical repairs and vehicle conversions. During the summer, we run an intensive programme that ensures every vehicle in our fleet of over 200 is inspected, serviced and certified ready for the new academic year.

## Planning for September

The goal of every summer maintenance programme is straightforward: when the first school run of September departs, every vehicle should be mechanically sound, fully compliant and ready for a full term of reliable service.

Achieving this requires planning that begins months in advance. Maintenance schedules are mapped against vehicle availability, parts are pre-ordered to avoid supply delays and staffing in the workshop is scaled to meet the summer workload.

For councils and commissioners, the quality of a provider's summer maintenance programme is a strong indicator of their overall operational capability. A provider that invests heavily in preventative maintenance during the summer is far less likely to experience the service disruptions that undermine confidence during the school year.

The work that happens in the workshop during July and August may not be visible to parents and students, but it underpins every reliable journey that follows.
